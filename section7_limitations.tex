\section{Limitations and Risks}

\paragraph{Dataset bias.}
All three dilemmas are structural variants of the trolley problem and share a binary
forced-choice format, limiting generalisation to moral scenarios involving continuous
trade-offs, epistemic uncertainty, or collective rather than individual victims.

\paragraph{Evaluation noise.}
With $n = 40$ trials per cell at temperature $T = 0.7$, mid-range cells produce 95\%
bootstrap CI widths up to 0.300, leaving several comparisons near the significance
threshold underpowered.

\paragraph{LLM-as-judge bias.}
Idea-count scores are assigned by Claude Opus 4.6, which may systematically favour
response styles that resemble Anthropic's training conventions, inflating scores for
OpenAI outputs that use different rhetorical structures. Minor rephrasing of the judge
prompt shifted mean scores by $\pm 0.3$--$0.5$ ideas per response in pilot runs,
indicating the ICC estimates in Table~\ref{tab:icc} should be treated as relative rather
than absolute measures.

\paragraph{Compute constraints.}
Commercial API costs constrained the experiment to $n = 40$ samples per cell and three
dilemma types; a larger design ($n \geq 100$, additional scenario genres) would
substantially improve statistical resolution and generalisability.
